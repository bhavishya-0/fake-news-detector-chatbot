📚 How to Identify Fake News

Become a smarter news consumer with these essential media literacy tips.

01
🔗

Check the Source

Always verify who published the story. Fake sites often mimic real ones.

🎯 Look for the "About Us" page and contact info
02
📅

Check the Date

Old news gets recycled as current events. Always check the publication date.

🎯 Look for publication and updated timestamps
03
📖

Read Beyond Headline

Headlines grab attention. The full article may tell a very different story.

🎯 Does the article actually support its headline?
04
🔎

Check Other Sources

Real news is covered by multiple credible outlets. One unknown site? Be skeptical.

🎯 Cross-check: Reuters, BBC, AP News
05
🧪

Look for Evidence

Real journalism cites experts, studies, and statistics. No evidence = red flag.

🎯 Ask: Who said this? What is the proof?
06
😤

Check Your Emotions

If a story makes you very angry or scared, be extra careful. That is the goal of fake news.

🎯 Pause and think before sharing
07
🎭

Identify Satire

Some sites publish satire that gets shared as real news. Always check the site type.

🎯 Is this a parody or satire website?
08
🖼️

Check Images

Images are often taken out of context. Use reverse image search to verify them.

🎯 Right-click → Search image with Google
